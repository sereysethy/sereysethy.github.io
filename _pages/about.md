### Research & Technical Background

During my PhD, I developed **ASGARD** (Adaptive Self-Guarded Autonomous Response and Deception), an abstract model for creating adaptive honeypot systems. ASGARD's flexible architecture allows for customization across different use cases, communication protocols, and deployment scenarios—enabling security teams to build deception systems tailored to their specific threat environments.

Beyond academic research, I bring substantial **industry experience in software development and technical leadership**. I previously led a team of 10+ developers in building a complex ledger system and multimedia authoring platform, giving me hands-on expertise in algorithms, data structures, and the full software development lifecycle. This combination of research depth and practical engineering experience allows me to bridge the gap between cutting-edge security concepts and real-world implementation.

**Research Interests:**
* Intrusion detection and deception systems using adaptive honeypots
* Applied machine learning in cybersecurity, including reinforcement learning for threat response
